System Overview

What DocuMark
actually does

DocuMark is a writing transparency tool — it makes your composing process legible in ways traditional submission cannot. Before you decide whether to use it, you should know exactly what it sees.

⌨ Keystrokes, pauses, and revision patterns as you write
⧉ Copy-paste events and pasted text volume
◈ Generates an effort score and an ownership score
◎ Estimates percentage of AI-generated vs. human-written text

What it cannot see: what you're thinking. Whether you struggled. Whether you learned. Those remain yours. // Data will not be used punitively. Full disclosure provided before you begin.

Why This Matters

Transparency is never neutral.
Neither is its absence.

Shoshana Zuboff

Behavioral data doesn't just record what you do — it predicts and shapes future behavior. Who benefits from the data? Who sets the terms?

Intellectual Privacy

Even the possibility of surveillance exerts a chilling effect on thought, speech, and creativity — before any data is collected at all.

The Panopticon

Not a metaphor. A functional description of what any monitoring system does: it makes the writer aware of being seen, and that awareness changes the writing.

This Exploration doesn't ask you to render a verdict on DocuMark. It asks an honest question: What are the trade-offs of process transparency — and what do those trade-offs reveal about intellectual privacy, student agency, and academic integrity?
