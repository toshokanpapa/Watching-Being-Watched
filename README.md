What does it mean to write when your process is visible? DocuMark is designed as a writing transparency tool — it makes the composing process legible in ways that traditional submission cannot. But transparency, like any design choice, involves trade-offs. In this optional multi-week Exploration, you will examine those trade-offs from the inside. You and your fellow Seekers will choose between two writing paths: one uses DocuMark to compose an essay on process transparency and academic integrity; the other uses a non-DocuMark workflow to write on the same theme. Both paths lead to the same central question:
What are the trade-offs of process transparency — and what do those trade-offs reveal about intellectual privacy, student agency, and academic integrity?
Along the way you will engage Shoshana Zuboff's account of surveillance capitalism, scholarship on intellectual privacy, and your own lived experience of writing when your process is — or isn't — visible to others. This Exploration asks you to be both analyst and subject: to generate genuine evidence about how process transparency shapes thought, voice, and trust.
The concept of surveillance appears in this Exploration as a critical lens drawn from our course frameworks — a tool for analyzing how visibility functions in any system, not a characterization of DocuMark itself. Our goal is not to render a verdict on the tool but to ask an honest, rigorous question about what transparency costs and what it offers — and to let your experience be part of the answer.
